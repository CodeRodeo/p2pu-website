Learning Circles Facilitator Guide

Learning Circles are lightly-facilitated study groups for learners who want to take freely available online courses together, in-person. We believe that anybody can become a facilitator, and whether you want to run a single Learning Circle in your home or organize 100 across an urban library system, this guide is here to help you get going.

You can run a Learning Circle with just this guide and an online course, but we highly recommend you create your Learning Circle using our free software tools. Our web app will generate a Learning Circle website where learners can signup and create a facilitator dashboard, where you can communicate directly with learners, set up automatic reminders to go out before each Learning Circle, and share weekly feedback with learners, P2PU, and other facilitators.

A few things before we begin

Use our tools. Throughout this guide, you’ll see teal highlights where our software can support your Learning Circle.

Let us help. P2PU can consult individuals and organizations looking to get started with Learning Circles. Our services include facilitator training workshops, course selection and adaption expertise, ongoing facilitator support, web hosting, and software tool integration. We are happy to have a 15 minute call with you to talk about strategy and answer any questions you might have - email thepeople@p2pu.org to get that set up.

Share your stories. We rely on input and participation from the community, and your feedback will make Learning Circles even better going forward. Head over to community.p2pu.org to share feedback, photos, suggestions, and questions with facilitators from around the world.

READ THIS GUIDE. The first part - Background - provides introductory information about Learning Circles and the type of learning that the format aims to develop. The second part - Getting Started - includes the steps you need to check off before beginning. The third part - Running a Learning Circle - introduces facilitation tips and the guidelines for the routine you'll develop each week. The final part - Downloads - includes a PDF version of this handbook, as well as additional tools and templates you can download as needed. >
